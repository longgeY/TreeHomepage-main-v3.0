Corylus americana or the American Filbert is also known as the American Hazel. It is one of three species of Corylus that occur in North America, and one of two that is native to Minnesota. Corylus is the classical Latin name for hazel, possibly derived from the Greek korylos or corys, a helmet, in reference to the husk that surrounds the nut. American Filbert is a relatively large shrub with numerous erect stems and is important in Minnesotan forest ecology because of its abundance. In fact, it is considered a close second to beaked hazel (C. cornuta) as the most abundant understory shrub in Minnesota. The shrub provides structure and function of the forest understory and influences succession of canopy trees.
